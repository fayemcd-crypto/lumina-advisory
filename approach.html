Our approach

Transformation fails.

Most organisations know what needs to change. They have the strategy, the budget, the consultants. What they don't have is a way through the resistance. That's where Lumina comes in.

The problem

The adoption gap

Organisations invest billions every year in transformation — new strategies, new technology, new operating models. And yet most of it never truly lands. The programmes get funded, the consultants deliver, the decks get presented. And then nothing really changes.

This is the adoption gap — the space between intention and action, between what an organisation says it stands for and what it actually does. It's not a strategy problem. It's not a technology problem. It's a human one.

And it lives in the resistance.

70%

£900bn

Why transformation fails

The resistance is always
hiding in plain sight

Systems protect themselves
Governance, incentives and reward loops are designed for the world that existed — not the one you're trying to build. The structure actively resists the change it's supposed to enable.
Power distorts truth
Hierarchy silences honest challenge. The people who can see the problems most clearly are rarely in the room where decisions get made — and if they are, they don't feel safe to speak.
Change lands on people, not with them
Transformation is handed down, not built together. People aren't prepared, supported or heard through the process — so they resist, disengage, or simply wait for it to pass.

The Resistance Framework

Illuminating the Resistance

Lumina's framework maps the four domains where resistance lives in every organisation. When you can see it clearly, you can move through it. When these four domains align, transformation becomes inevitable.

01
Systems & Governance
Where structural resistance hides
The architecture of an organisation — its governance structures, operating models, incentives and reward loops — either enables change or actively resists it. We diagnose and redesign the systems that are holding transformation back.
02
Power & Politics
Where hierarchical resistance plays out
Power dynamics, psychological safety and the unspoken rules of hierarchy shape every decision an organisation makes. We map the power landscape and help leaders understand how to navigate it with integrity.
03
Judgement & Decision
Where cognitive resistance operates
Under pressure, even capable leaders make poor decisions. Bias, overconfidence and cognitive shortcuts distort judgement at the moments that matter most. We develop the clarity and decision integrity to cut through the noise.
04
Resilience & Regulation
Where human resistance shows up
Change is physiologically threatening. The nervous system responds to uncertainty the same way it responds to danger. We build the emotional maturity and self-awareness that allows leaders to respond rather than react.
